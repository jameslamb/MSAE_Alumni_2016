Economics in the Age of Big Data

From Einav & Levin (2014):

  • Economists increasingly using large data sets (private & administrative)

    "Economic models bring a simplifying conceptual framework to to help make sense of large data sets"

  • A major challenge:

    "...developing appropriate data management and programming capabilities, as well as designing creative and scalable approaches to summarize, describe, and analyze...data sets"

Appendix - Key Academic Papers

The resources listed here have shaped my view of the near-term future of economic thought and the economics profession. I hope you find them as interesting and useful as I did.

  • Arthur (2014). Complexity and the Economy.
  • Cagle (2014). Ontology for Fun and Profit.
  • Datta (2014). Future IoT.
  • Datta (n.d.). WiFi Meet FuFi: Disruptive Innovation in Logistics Catalysed by Energy.
  • Einav & Levin (2014). Economics in the age of big data.
  • Hausmann & Hidalgo, et. al (2011). The Atlas of Economic Complexity.
  • Kahneman (2013). Thinking, Fast and Slow.
  • Varian (2014). Beyond Big Data.
  • Varian (2013). Big Data: New Tricks for Econometrics.
